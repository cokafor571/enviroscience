What you need to know about Asbestos

Asbestos is a carcinogen that can be found in buildings. When asbestos-containing materials otherwise known as ACM are damaged, fibers containing asbestos can be get into the air which can potentially be very hazardous for occupants.

Common misconceptions about Asbestos

A visual inspection is not sufficient to determine the existence of asbestos. One can only test for asbestos with laboratory testing of the damaged material which is accomplished by an Asbestos Survey or Screening. It is absolutely vital that building owners or purchasers know whether a building has ACM so that it can be taken care of properly. After identification, asbestos can be safely sealed and effectively removed. We provide these services to our clients.

What you need to know about Lead

Lead was widely used in paint for residential and commercial buildings before 1978. However, lead is toxic for both humans and animals and lead poisoning is somewhat common in young children. Adults are also susceptible to lead poisoning under a few conditions as well. While “do it yourself” lead inspection kits are available, they are by no means 100% reliable. We can minimize your risk by bringing in our professional team who can test and remove any lead based paint that you may have on the property.

What you need to know about Soil

Soil testing, or a soil analysis, is usually required for building permits to ensure that there is no contaminants in the ground. A soil analysis can be ordered by a home buyer or real estate agent to learn of any potential hazards that could adversely affect their home and investment. For instance, a vast majority of home buyers are not prepared for all the complications of hillside living, including erosion, gullies, and landslides caused by intense winter rains. Soil testing by our licensed professionals can make homeowners aware of the some of the risks involved in such homes and we also make them away of potential courses of action that can safegaurd them against such pitfalls going forward.
